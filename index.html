A Confession For Anyone Who Lands Here.

You have landed here at an interesting time. I know for a fact that all statements of purpose, curriculum vitaes and documents for application to Ph.D. programs need to be of a professional nature. But as long as you are here, I will give myself some slack and be a little unprofessional.
I know that I haven't studied in the best of places for my undergraduate degree. I know that I could have been better all along. But I did the best and everything that I have experienced contributes to my knowledge and my perspectives of the world.
You see, in India, all of the half-decent engineering colleges have really serious on-campus placements. I got an on-campus job at MuSigma. That was the one and only on-campus job placement that I had taken a shot at. I got the job and I never looked back. About 10 months down the line, MuSigma called me to ask if I wanted to join them, but I put their offer down.
MuSigma is an amazing company. But my dreams were bigger than me. I gave up a high paying job for about $150 a month - to pursue research in PESIT Bangalore South Campus. Heck, I earn that much tutoring for 14 hours a week, for 10 days. But evidently, I had to give up a lot more so that I could follow my dreams.
But that's what I did. I worked with Dr. Snehanshu Saha, my academic mentor (and his opinions will matter the most to me for many many years to come). I settled for a really low-paying job so that I could do what I love - work on problems in research. Yes. One full year. Twelve full months. 365+ days.
And you might stop to wonder - why did I mention the '+'? That's because once I decide something is important in my life, I pursue it relentlessly. Dr. Saha and I set up the Astroinformatics Research Group. What started as a set of scribbles on a whiteboard, over arguments of what we should name ourselves. We finally decided to call ourselves AstrIRG.
